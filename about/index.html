About --help

Currently working as a Data scientist, I would like to shift my career path and become a penetration tester.
When I decided to learn more about cybersecurity, I didn't really know where to start. I had very basic knowledge of how networks work, but I didn't know much about hacking.
A friend of mine talked to me about a course on a site called Coursera. Apparently, it consisted in building a computer and was very well rated. A few days later, we were in. We were not disappointed... The course was super fun and interesting (you can see more here).
I really enjoyed the fact that it was very practical and low level. It also introduced me to Coursera, where I would find plenty of courses on cybersecurity.

I was very excited when I started a self-paced course costing 40$/month (the quicker you finish, the less you pay). I was motivated and thought I would finish it within 2-3 months.
10 months later, I was still paying for that course. I couldn't find the motivation to continue : it was too much theory, not enough practice.
I could spend hours watching videos and reading assignments, yet when I thought "what could I actually do, could I hack something, ...", nothing came to my mind. Furthermore, the course recommended to buy the book "TCP/IP Illustrated" by W. Richard and we were supposed to read a chapter each week. This is what achieved me. The course was a beginner level, but this book is basically the experienced network administrator's bible. I had to read each sentence several times to barely understand what is was talking about. I cancelled my subscription and stopped for a few months.
When my motivation came back, I decided I had to find something practical. After all, like many others, I learn best by doing.
After some research, I found Vulnhub and Hack The Box. I also looked for serious certifications that could help me shifting my carreer path and found those of Offensive Security. I was pretty much ready to go, but I still felt like I needed a little theory. WIP
